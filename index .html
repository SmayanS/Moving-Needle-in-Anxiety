Moving the Needle in Anxiety


Currently there is a stigma around all mental health disorders especially anxiety because people don't want to talk about it due to its negative connotation in the past. This causes people to struggle silently since they don’t have anyone to talk to. If you feel like you have a mental health condition or you want to talk to someone that does, please take a look at the resources below

What is Anxiety and how can I treat it?

As we know now mental health is a serious topic in the world right now, and can lead to serious consequences, one mental health illness is an anxiety disorder. While we all do feel anxiety, when it is consistently affecting you and getting in the way of your daily life, you might have an anxiety disorder. If you feel that your anxiety is permanent and is getting worse you might have a disorder. However, there are a lot of resources and people that can help those who are struggling. If you feel that you are struggling with any mental illness the most important thing is to talk to someone you trust about your current situation. Opening up to someone will get you to talk about your feelings, which can lead to your deeper understanding about your condition. Additionally, you should see a therapist or professional for help regarding your anxiety. Even if it doesn’t seem like much at the time, seeing a professional early will make the treatment process better. Having an opinion from an expert in the field will surely eventually improve your current mental status.

How to communicate with someone who has Anxiety

For a lot of people talking to someone with a mental health condition such as anxiety might be intimidating due to all the stigma surrounding all of mental health. However, it is important to understand that these people are still people, even though they have something else going on. When you are talking to one of these individuals it’s important to take some time to hear them out and let them share as much as they want. You don’t want to try to diagnose them since they are already talking to a professional or still trying to understand what they are feeling and you might get in the way. You want to create a safe environment and express your continued support so they feel that this is a trusted space where they can share again. It is important to treat them as if they are anyone else, since they are already feeling left out and you don’t want to add on to that further. However if the situation turns dire and they talk of harming themselves or other it important to contact authorities since safety is the first priority.

Other kinds of mental disorders

There are many different types of mental illness, each with a variety of symptoms and effects. It is important to note that these diseases are very serious and can have a lasting effect on an individual's quality of life.

  • Bipolar Disorder: A person with bipolar disorder will experience dramatic changes in their mood or energy without any reason.
  • Depressive Disorder: This is more than just being sad, it can affect the individual's mood, actions, and thoughts
  • ADHD: The attention deficit hyperactivity disorder is when an individual has hyperactivity, inattention, or impulsivity
  • PTSD: Post traumatic stress disorder is when a person's mental health is affected by a traumatic event in the past
  • Eating Disorder: An eating disorder is when a person is so concerned about food or their weight that they can’t focus on other things

These are just some of the many mental health conditions that you might have even though there are so many more. I highly recommend doing specific research into your situation and looking into the other sections.
